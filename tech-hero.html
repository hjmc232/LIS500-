Tech Hero: Lola Odelola

Founder of Blackgirl.tech and advocate for equity in technology

About Lola

Lola Odelola is a software engineer, campaigner, and founder of the non-profit Blackgirl.tech. The organization is dedicated to increasing representation for Black women and non-binary people in technology. Odelola graduated from Kingston University in 2013 with a background in creative writing. However, she pivoted to coding after creating a website from scratch to showcase her writing. Today, she is on the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) Technical Architecture Group (TAG) where she focuses on improving the accessibility of website architecture.

Why I Chose Lola

Lola's is a success story for People of Color in technology that speaks to her dedication to bettering her communities. I resonate with her backstory of pivoting into tech from another background as I am performing the same jump from public policy. I am inspired by her leadership to open Blackgirl.tech especially as so few women, non-binary persons, and people of color are represented in the tech industry. While the non-profit is currently on hiatus, Lola never stopped creating good in tech as she now works with TAG, and founded her next project, Lola's Lab. This resonates with me as my particular user experience interests include accessibility and information architecture. Her driven spirit inspires me because she has impressively moved the line in the sand for what is achievable by young technologists.
