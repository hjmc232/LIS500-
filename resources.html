Terms to know

Implicit Bias

Implict bias is the negative, unconscious way sterotypes and attitudes are formed that may be ingrained in a person without them realizing. These prejeduices may impact the decisions, behaviors, and perceptions about different racial groups, social, or gender groups. It is important to acknowledge your implict bias and counteract them.

Intersectionality

Intersectionality is the way two or more social identities (e.g. living the experience of a Black female in the United States) may compound to create increased levels of oppression that would be absent if the social identities individually experienced the same situation.

Harvard University's IAT (Implicit Association Test)

Project Implicit was developed is Harvard Univeristy to examine if individual's find it easier to pair attribute with one group of people compared to another. For example, do you associate the term "science" more with the name "Jared" or "Monique"? The IAT can be used thoughtfuly to understand your implicit biases so you can intentionally acknowledge and take action to dispell them.

Why this is Important to You

The University of Wisconsin-Madison is a diverse campus with students, faculty, and staff from around the world. During your first few years as a Badger, you may encounter new cultures and identities unfamilar to you. It is critical that you acknowledge your implict bias, and welcome peers, teammates, and professors, among others, with an open mind. As you join student organizations, choose study groups, and form friendships, it is Important to understand the experiences and biases those around you face. It makes you a better classmate, leader, and member of the community.
