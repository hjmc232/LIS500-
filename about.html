About Me

My name is Hunter (he/him) and I am a second-year Master of Public Affairs student, civic technologist, and creative.

Background

I was born and raised in Ashland, Kentucky, a small town in Eastern Kentucky close to Ohio and West Virginia. I studied economics and public policy at the University of Kentucky. After graduation I worked at Deloitte consulting where I focused on improving and modernizing Commonwealth of Kentucky government systems. It was here that I was attracted to working in technology, which was a departure from my previous interests in health and housing policy. I'm interested in this class because in order to make better user experiences for all citizens, we must consider the power dynamics and representation in tech. I also want to learn technical front-end development in order to better articulate design choices.

My interests

  • Digital accessibility and digital design
  • Artificial Intelligence usage in government
  • Human centered-design and community research
